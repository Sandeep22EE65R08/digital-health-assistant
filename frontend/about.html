About the project

Turning confusing medical jargon into calm, clear answers

The AI Digital Health Assistant is a free, open-source tool that helps everyday people understand their symptoms and lab reports in plain language — English, Hindi, or Hinglish. Powered by Google's Gemini AI, it reads PDFs, scanned reports and even phone photos of paper test results, then explains every value with its normal range and what it could mean. Built so you walk into the doctor's office with better questions, not more fear.

100% free forever Open source 3 languages 0 accounts required

What it can do for you

Explain your symptoms

Describe how you feel and get a structured breakdown — possible causes, severity, red-flag warnings, suggested specialist, useful tests and simple home-care steps.

Decode lab reports

Upload a PDF or a phone photo of a printed report. Every parameter is mapped to its normal range, abnormal values are flagged, and you get a friendly summary of what they might mean.

Read handwriting & scans

Gemini's vision model reads scanned PDFs and phone photos directly — including handwritten reference ranges. No need to retype anything.

Follow-up like ChatGPT

Ask "what does this value mean?" or "should I worry?" — the assistant remembers context so each answer builds on the last conversation.

Why this exists

Most people in India and beyond get a lab report or feel a new symptom and end up doom-scrolling vague search results, jumping between WhatsApp forwards, or paying for a consultation just to be told "your numbers look fine."

This assistant fills the awkward gap between Google and your doctor — a calm, structured first read in your own language, available any time of day, that helps you understand what's on the paper before you act on it.

It will never replace a doctor. But it can make the visit far more productive.

Honest about limits

✅ What it is

  • An educational AI explainer
  • A second opinion on what numbers mean
  • A way to prep better questions for your doctor
  • Free, open-source, audit-able

❌ What it is not

  • A diagnosis or prescription
  • A substitute for a qualified clinician
  • For use in emergencies
  • A store of your medical history (nothing is saved)

🚨 In an emergency

Don't use this app. Call your local emergency number immediately: India: 112 · UK: 999 · US: 911. Red-flag signs include chest pain, trouble breathing, signs of stroke, severe bleeding, or sudden severe headache.
